Volume Profile

📊 What is Volume Profile?

Volume Profile is a popularity map for price. Instead of showing how much was traded over time (like a regular volume bar), it shows how much was traded at each price level.

Think of it like a building: the widest floors had the most activity — those are prices where traders agreed value exists. The narrow floors are prices traders rushed through without much interest.

  • Wide bars = heavy trading = strong support or resistance
  • Thin bars = light trading = price moves fast through these levels

🔷 The 4 Market Shapes

The VP card analyzes price relative to the Value Area (the zone where 70% of trading happened) and assigns one of four shapes:

D Range — Sideways Price is stuck between a ceiling and floor. No breakout yet.
➜ Buy near the floor (VAL), sell near the ceiling (VAH)
P Bullish — Up Trend Price has broken above the ceiling. Bulls in control.
➜ Look for pullbacks to buy, follow the trend
b Bearish — Down Trend Price has broken below the floor. Bears in control.
➜ Look for bounces to sell, don't fight the trend
B Dual Zone — Volatile Two separate trading zones formed. Price swings between them.
➜ Wait for a clear direction before entering

📍 Reading the Chart

The histogram on the card shows volume at each price. Here's what the colored levels mean:

Name What it tells you
POC Point of Control — the single busiest price. Acts like a magnet: price often returns here. Key level for entries and targets.
VAH Value Area High — the ceiling of "fair value." Above this, price is expensive relative to recent activity. Breakout point for bulls.
VAL Value Area Low — the floor of "fair value." Below this, price is cheap. Support level and breakout point for bears.
HVN High Volume Node — a thick price zone with heavy trading. Strong support or resistance. Price often pauses here.
LVN Low Volume Node — a thin zone with little trading. Price moves fast through these gaps. Useful for setting targets.

🧠 How Traders Use This Card

1 Check the Shape — D, P, b, or B tells you the current market personality. Is it ranging? Trending up? Trending down? Confused?
2 Look at the State — Acceptance means price is inside fair value (normal). Rejection means price broke out and stayed out — a move may be starting.
3 Read the Pills — Strategy Bias (FADE, FOLLOW, WAIT) is your cheat sheet. Probability Tier tells you how confident the signal is. AMT Lockout warns if the aggressive trading bot is active.
4 Use the Levels — POC is your magnet. VAH/VAL are your boundaries. The Trade Setup block gives you a concrete plan: entry, targets (T1, T2), stop, and size.

❓ Common Questions

What does "Value Area" mean?

The Value Area is the price zone where 70% of all trading volume happened during the session. Think of it as the "fair price range" — where most traders agreed value belongs.

Why does price return to the POC?

The POC is where the most volume traded — the most buyers and sellers agreed on price there. Markets have a memory: when price drifts away, it often gets pulled back to retest this level. Traders call this the "magnet effect."

When should I ignore the card?

During major news events (Fed announcements, CPI data, etc.), volume profiles from before the event become stale. The B-Shape (Dual Zone) also signals uncertainty — the card may flip shapes quickly. In these cases, wait for a new profile to form.

How often does the data update?

The card refreshes every 60 seconds automatically. Each session creates a new volume profile, so levels reset daily around the New York open (9:30 AM ET).
