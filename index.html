📊 What is Volume Profile?

Volume Profile is a popularity map for price. Instead of showing how much was traded over time (like a regular volume bar), it shows how much was traded at each price level.

Think of it like a building: the widest floors had the most activity — those are prices where traders agreed value exists. The narrow floors are prices traders rushed through without much interest.

  • Wide bars = heavy trading = strong support or resistance (value area)
  • Thin bars = light trading = price moves fast through these levels (low activity gap)

🔷 The 4 Market Shapes

The VP card analyzes price relative to the Value Area (the zone where 70% of trading happened) and assigns one of four shapes based on the volume profile curve:

D Balanced Range (D-shape) Volume is centered in the middle. Price is stuck between a ceiling and floor.
➜ Buy near the floor (VAL), sell near the ceiling (VAH), fade to the center.
P Bullish Trend (P-shape) Volume is concentrated at the top. Price has broken above the ceiling.
➜ Buy pullbacks, ride breakout momentum in control of bulls.
b Bearish Trend (b-shape) Volume is concentrated at the bottom. Price has broken below the floor.
➜ Sell rallies, ride breakdown momentum in control of bears.
B Double Range (B-shape) Two separate trading zones have formed with a gap between them.
➜ Trade each zone independently, wait for a breakout from the middle gap.

📍 Reading the Chart

The vertical histogram shows volume at each price level (highest price at top, lowest at bottom). Here's what the colored levels mean:

Name What it tells you
POC Point of Control (Busiest Price) — the single busiest price. Acts like a magnet: price often returns here. Key level for entries and targets.
VAH Value Area High (Price Ceiling) — the upper ceiling of "fair value." Above this, price is expensive. Breakout point for bulls.
VAL Value Area Low (Price Floor) — the lower floor of "fair value." Below this, price is cheap. Support level and breakdown point for bears.
HVN High Volume Node (High Activity Zone) — a thick price zone with heavy trading. Strong support or resistance where price often pauses.
LVN Low Volume Node (Low Activity Gap) — a thin zone with little trading. Price moves fast through these gaps. Useful for setting targets.

❓ Common Questions

What does "Value Area" mean?

The Value Area is the price zone where 70% of all trading volume happened during the session. Think of it as the "fair price range" — where most buyers and sellers agreed transaction value belongs.

Why does price return to the POC?

The POC is the single busiest level. Markets have memory: when price drifts away, it often gets pulled back to retest the highest-liquidity level before moving on. Traders call this the "magnet effect."

What is the "Bot Active" warning?

When an automated trading bot (executing on Aggressive Market Tape) is highly active, price action can become extremely volatile and erratic. The card automatically detects this and suggests skipping or halving trade sizing to protect capital.

How often does the data update?

The card refreshes every 60 seconds automatically. Each session resets levels daily around the New York open (9:30 AM ET).
